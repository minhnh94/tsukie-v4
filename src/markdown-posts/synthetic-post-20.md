## The Virtue of Deliberateness

Speed is celebrated in software. Velocity. Iteration cycles. Move fast. Ship daily.

Nobody talks about the value of slowing down.

When I was a junior developer, I wrote code quickly. I also introduced a lot of bugs. The bugs cost ten times as long to fix as they would have taken to avoid.

The most experienced engineers I have worked with share a common trait: they think before they type. They ask questions before they code. They question assumptions before they design.

> Fast fingers produce slow software. Slow thinking produces fast software.

This is not an argument for waterfall planning. It is an argument for thoughtfulness. Thirty seconds of consideration before writing a function saves hours of debugging later.
