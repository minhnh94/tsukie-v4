## Complexity Compounds

There is a cruel irony in software: the more features you ship, the harder it becomes to ship the next feature.

Each addition creates a web of interactions. Edge cases multiply combinatorially. Your test suite balloons. Onboarding new users becomes harder.

The best products I have ever used are not the ones with the most features. They are the ones that do one thing extraordinarily well.

> "Perfection is achieved not when there is nothing more to add, but when there is nothing left to take away." — Antoine de Saint-Exupéry

This applies to software as much as it applies to prose.
