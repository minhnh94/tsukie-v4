## The Math of Consistency

1% better every day for a year is 37x better by the end of the year. 1% worse every day is 0.03 of what you started with.

The math is extreme, but the principle holds at smaller scales. Consistent daily effort in any skill creates exponential improvement that sporadic bursts of intensity cannot match.

I published 180 blog posts in the last year. Not all of them were good. Some were actively embarrassing. But the 180th post is measurably better than the first, and the skills I built writing them compound into everything I build.

```
The trajectory of a consistent creator:

Months 1-3:  Nothing seems to happen. Most people quit here.
Months 4-6:  Small signs of momentum. Keep going.
Months 7-9:  Things start compounding. You can feel it.
Months 10+:  The gap between you and where you started is unmistakable.
```

Show up every day. Do the small thing. Trust the math.
