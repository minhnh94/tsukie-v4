## The Warning Signs I Ignored

I thought I was just tired. I had been building for 18 months without a real break. Of course I was tired.

Burnout is different. The first sign is that work that used to feel energizing now feels neutral. Then mildly aversive. Then something you have to force yourself to start.

The second sign is that you cannot enjoy rest. I took a week off and spent it scrolling, unable to read, unable to focus on anything. My brain had lost the ability to be idle without anxiety.

> You cannot productivity-hack your way out of burnout. Rest is not a reward for finishing your tasks. It is a maintenance requirement.

Recovery took three months of deliberately under-working. It was the most productive investment I have ever made.
