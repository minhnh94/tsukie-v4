## The Underpricing Trap

Almost every indie hacker underprices their first product. I charged $5/month when competitors charged $29. I thought cheap would attract customers.

It did not. It signaled low quality.

When I raised prices to $19/month, conversions went up. When I raised to $29, they went up again. There seems to be a psychological floor below which people assume the product is not serious.

```
My pricing evolution:
v1: $5/mo  → 3 customers (embarrassed to sell at this price)
v2: $9/mo  → 11 customers (still too cheap)
v3: $19/mo → 34 customers (this felt right)
v4: $29/mo → 41 customers (sweet spot)
v5: $39/mo → 38 customers (slightly too high for this market)
```

The data was clear. I stayed at $29. The lesson: price at the edge of what makes you uncomfortable.
